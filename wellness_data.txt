Palakkad is a blessed land that boasts of lush green paddy fields, mist-
drenched mountain ranges, dense forest ranges where rare flora and fauna
flourish and even majestic monuments that stand as testimonies to various
by-gone eras. Apart from being a land where you can relax and rejuvenate
leisurely, Palakkad is also rich in heritage and culture. Some of the most
influential and popular art forms in Kerala like Kathakali and Carnatic music
are widely performed and taught throughout the region. Palakkad is also
home to many historic architectural marvels that have endured through
generations.
HIGHLIGHTS
Ayurveda program
Cultural programs
Yoga
--- Page 4 ---
Located near Kollangode in the Palghat District of North Kerala, India, and
fringed on one side by the majestic Western Ghat mountain ranges, Kalari
Kovilakom the 200-year-old palace, is where history meets natural
tranquillity. Belonging to the Vengunad chieftains, the Kalari Kovilakom
Palace was originally surrounded by land rich with Venga trees that have
healing properties. Set within the palace grounds of the old Vengunad
kingdom, Kalari Kovilakom offers 19 well-appointed rooms with modern
amenities and a clinic with 12 treatment rooms. The living spaces and the
gardens at Kalari Kovilakom complements the healing process with its
nurturing and warm environment and an unobtrusive, quiet adherence to
discipline.
Since the Vengunad kings were patrons of the classical arts ‐  especially
Kathakali dance and Carnatic music — such cultural programmes are an
essential part of the daily ritual at Kalari Kovilakom. Dance and music
performances, with the use of traditional Indian instruments such as
mrudangam, flute, violin, harmonium etc depicting mythological stories are
a way of life here as in early times.

everything on the beach goes at the same relaxed pace. Full of coconut and
palm trees, Gokarna is a 'one of a kind' place in the country.

Kerala’s famous backwaters are a labyrinthine network of tree-fringed
lagoons, idyllic lakes, and river systems, running parallel to the Arabian Sea
along the Malabar coast. Quaint towns and charming villages dot the banks

Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala's capital – still usually referred to by its
colonial-era name, Trivandrum – is a relatively compact but energetic city
spread across low-lying hills and is an easy-going introduction to urban
life down south. From a landscape naturally endowed with some of the
most picturesque visuals possible along with culturally relevant holy sites
that have thrived for more than a thousand years, this area has something
for everyone. Young and old alike, all can experience their own slice of
Kerala here, tailor-made to their individual preference.

Kalaripayattu, the compound word from Kalari (the sacred arena of practice
and performance) and Payattu (combat), is acknowledged as the mother of
all martial arts. With clear references in the 2500-year-old proto Tamil
Sangam literature of southern India, it is the oldest systematic training
system for the body and mind. Taken to China in the 4th Century by the
Pallava Prince turned Buddhist Monk Bodhidharma, who is revered around
the world even today in the form of Daruma doll, Kalaripayattu laid the
foundations of Shaolin, Karate and other renowned Asian martial arts. 
With a legacy of nearly 130 years, Agasthyam Kalari in Thiruvananthapuram,
the capital city of Kerala, has been custodians and educators of the southern
style of Kalaripayattu that considers the iconic Vedic seer and creator of
Tamil language, Sage Agasthya, as the Paramaguru or first teacher. With
several customized programs, Agasthyam has made Kalaripayattu accessible
to people of all ages around the world.

A fifth generation martial artist, Gurukkal Dr. S Mahesh, heads Agasthyam
Kalari . The title “Gurukkal” that is unique to Kalaripayattu is the plural
form of the familiar word Guru or master teacher. The plural form
represents the presence of the entire lineage of Parampara in the
contemporary Guru. Gurukkal is an expert in yoga, Vaastu and traditional
healing systems. 
A journalist before dedicating full time to the Kalari, he is well known as a
filmmaker, author and motivational speaker. With close to a million
followers on social media, Maheshji’s deep rooted knowledge and spirit of
innovation has led to Agasthyam beautifully blending classical mastery with
modern delivery.
