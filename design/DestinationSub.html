About India

The subcontinent that changes everything

India is not simply a destination — it is an experience that rewires the senses. A country of breathtaking contrasts: chaotic and serene, ancient and relentlessly modern, austere and spectacularly opulent. From the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas in the north to the palm-fringed backwaters of Kerala in the south, and from the Thar Desert in the west to the tea estates of Assam in the east, India's geography alone is staggering.

For the discerning traveler, India offers something that few destinations can match — an inexhaustible depth of culture, history, wildlife and landscape. Every region is essentially a separate country with its own language, cuisine, architecture and rhythm of life. AndThen designs private journeys that move through this complexity with grace and intention.

When to visit

Best time to travel to India

India is a vast country and the best time to visit depends on which region you're exploring. As a general rule, October through March offers the most comfortable conditions across most of the country — clear skies, mild temperatures and minimal rainfall.

For wildlife safaris in Central India, February through May is peak season when tigers are most active and visible. For Kerala and the south, October through February is ideal.

Season guide
Winter · Peak season October – March

Clear skies, mild temperatures and minimal rainfall across most of India. Ideal for the Golden Triangle, Rajasthan and cultural sightseeing. The Taj Mahal and heritage cities are at their finest.

Spring · Good season April – May

Warming temperatures in the north, but excellent for wildlife. Tiger sightings peak as animals congregate at waterholes. Kerala and the coastal south remain pleasant with sea breezes.

Monsoon · Not recommended June – September

Heavy rainfall across most of India. Most tiger reserves close. However, Kerala's landscapes are spectacularly lush, and Ayurveda retreats thrive in the monsoon humidity — ideal for deep wellness travel.
