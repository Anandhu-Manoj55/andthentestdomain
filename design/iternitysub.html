About this journey

India's greatest wildlife reserves, one extraordinary journey

India's tiger country is among the most thrilling wildlife destinations on Earth — and Wild Encounters takes you deep into the heart of it. This 15-night private journey moves through five of central India's most celebrated national parks, from the teeming forests of Madhya Pradesh to the dramatic landscapes of Maharashtra, before ending in the cultural grandeur of Delhi and Agra.

You begin in Mumbai — India's extraordinary port city — before venturing into tiger territory at Bandhavgarh, Kanha, Pench and Tadoba. Each reserve has its own character: Bandhavgarh for sheer tiger density, Kanha for the open meadows that inspired The Jungle Book, Pench for its pristine river corridor, and Tadoba for leopard sightings alongside its famous tigers.

Every safari is private. Every lodge has been personally inspected. This is wildlife travel done properly — unhurried, exclusive, and deeply immersive.

Kanha

The land that inspired Rudyard Kipling's Jungle Book. Kanha's sweeping meadows and dense sal forests are home to tigers, leopards and the rare barasingha deer — one of the finest wildlife experiences in India.

Exclusive private safaris Barasingha deer sanctuary

Named after the pristine Pench River that flows through it, this 758 sq km reserve offers some of the most atmospheric forest driving in central India alongside rich wildlife sightings.
