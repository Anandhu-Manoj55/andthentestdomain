The region

The most extraordinary region on earth, traveled privately

The Indian Subcontinent is not a single destination — it is a collection of worlds. India alone contains more cultural, geographical and culinary variety than most continents. Add Bhutan's intact Himalayan kingdom, Nepal's mountain trails and ancient valley cities, and Sri Lanka's ancient ruins, hill country and two distinct coastlines, and the scope becomes almost impossible to summarise.

AndThen works exclusively in this region. We know it in depth, not in breadth — every guide, every hotel, every route we recommend has been assessed firsthand. That knowledge is what we bring to designing your journey.

"The subcontinent rewards the traveler who goes slowly — who gives a place the time to reveal itself rather than demanding it perform on a schedule."

Private travel suits the Indian Subcontinent particularly well. The region's depth is not accessible on a group tour timetable. A morning at Varanasi's ghats, an afternoon tracking tigers in Bandhavgarh, a week in Bhutan's hill country without another foreign visitor in sight — these things require flexibility, a trusted local network, and a guide who is there for you alone.

AndThen designs journeys that are built around what you want to do — not around what is convenient to operate. Every itinerary on this site is a starting point. Nothing is fixed until you say so.

A note on combining countries

Many of our best journeys cross borders — India and Nepal, Bhutan and India, Sri Lanka with a detour to South India. We handle all permit logistics and cross-border arrangements as part of your itinerary. Tell us where you want to go and we will make the routing work.

02 Bhutan South Asia · 3 regions · 2 curated itineraries

The only carbon-negative country on earth — and one that measures progress in happiness, not GDP.

Bhutan limits tourist numbers deliberately. The result is a country where the dzongs are genuinely ancient, the monasteries are working religious centres, and the landscape has not been altered for visitors. A Sustainable Development Fee of $100 per person per night applies — AndThen handles the permit process in full.

  • Forest cover72%
  • Protected territory51% of land
  • Highest peakGangkhar Puensum 7,570m
  • Peak seasonMar–May · Sep–Nov
  • SDF fee$100 / person / night
What draws people
  • Taktsang — Tiger's Nest monastery, Paro
  • Punakha Dzong at the river confluence
  • Phobjikha valley — black-necked crane wintering
  • Bumthang — 7th-century temples in the hills
  • Spring rhododendron season

03 Nepal South Asia · 3 regions · 2 curated itineraries

Eight of the world's ten tallest peaks, ancient temples in the Kathmandu valley, and one of the finest wildlife parks in Asia.

Nepal is more varied than most visitors expect — the trekking routes are famous, but the medieval cities of the Kathmandu valley, the rhino safaris of Chitwan, and the lake city of Pokhara are equally compelling. We design private journeys that work for all levels of walker and all kinds of traveler.

04 Sri Lanka Indian Ocean · 3 regions · 3 curated itineraries

A compact island with 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, the highest leopard density in Asia, and two entirely different coastlines.

Sri Lanka fits more variety into 65,000 square kilometres than almost anywhere else — ancient capitals, a tea-covered highland, two UNESCO-listed natural reserves, Ayurveda on the south coast, and a blue whale corridor off Mirissa. It is also the most manageable South Asian destination for a first-time regional visitor.

  • UNESCO sites8
  • Coastline1,340 km
  • Recorded history2,500 years
  • Peak seasonDec–Mar (south)
  • Visa requiredETA available online
What draws people
  • Sigiriya — 5th-century rock fortress
  • Galle Fort and the south coast
  • Yala — highest wild leopard density in Asia
  • The Kandy to Ella train through tea country
  • Blue whale watching off Mirissa (Dec–Apr)
