About the hotel

A palace that floats on water

Built in 1746 as a pleasure palace for Maharana Jagat Singh II, the Taj Lake Palace occupies its own island in the middle of Lake Pichola. White marble courtyards, intricate jali screens, hand-painted alcoves and gardens blooming with lotus flowers conspire to make reality feel slightly unreal. Access is by boat only — the city falls away the moment you step off the jetty.

The hotel has 83 rooms and suites, three dining venues including Jharokha — one of the finest restaurants in Rajasthan — and a spa set in a courtyard garden. Sunsets from the Bhairo bar over the lake are, quite simply, extraordinary.
